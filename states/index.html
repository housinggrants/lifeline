Why state selection matters

Lifeline eligibility has national rules, but provider availability and device offers can vary by state and ZIP code. A provider that appears in one state may not serve another, and a device offer shown in an advertisement may not be available once a user chooses a state. Selecting your state first helps keep the next step realistic.

This state hub does not collect your address. It simply helps route you to a result page where you can compare provider categories, documents, and warnings.

After choosing a state

Read the result page carefully. Then compare providers, review documents needed, and check common application mistakes before opening a provider application. If your main goal is a free iPhone or free tablet, verify device stock and terms on the provider page near the end of your research.

FAQ

Does every provider serve every state?

No. Provider availability varies by state, ZIP code, eligibility route, and current provider participation.

Should I choose state before provider?

Yes. State availability can change which provider and device options make sense.

Does the state page approve me?

No. It only points you to the right next step. Final approval is handled by the provider, National Verifier, or state process where applicable.
